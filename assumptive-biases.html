Assumptive Biases

Pre-orientations of perception that shape how experience is entered before it unfolds. These biases act as silent lenses, influencing interpretation, tempo, and depth of engagement.

Overview

Assumptive biases establish the initial contour of experience. They are not distortions alone— they are shaping forces that determine how the field opens, contracts, or harmonizes at entry.

Types of Assumptive Biases

Negative Pre-Assumption

Entry through expectation of failure, conflict, or deficiency. This bias reduces perceptual openness and pre-activates tension structures.

Criticism Entry

Orientation through immediate evaluation or fault-finding. Perception narrows to detect mismatch rather than movement.

Harmonic Bias

Entry by assuming inherent coherence or alignment. This creates fluidity but may obscure foundational detail.

Coherence Entry

Entering through assumed structural order. It stabilizes perception but can create rigidity in interpretation.

Glorification Bias

Entry through idealization, amplification, or elevation of the other. It expands affect rapidly but distorts appraisal and tempo.

Function in the Field

Assumptive biases determine how one arrives in experience. They influence tempo, relational coupling, perceptual aperture, and the capacity to sustain clarity.
